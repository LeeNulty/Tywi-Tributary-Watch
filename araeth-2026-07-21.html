Summary

The River Araeth survey recorded favourable flow and excellent habitat diversity. The biological sample contained a diverse assemblage of pollution-sensitive macroinvertebrates, supported by water chemistry results showing low measured nutrient concentrations.

Interpretation

The BMWP score of 75 and ASPT of 7.5, together with the presence of mayflies, stoneflies and caddisflies, indicate a diverse community typical of clean, well-oxygenated water. The report notes little evidence of organic pollution at the surveyed site.

This represents one sampling event and provides a valuable baseline for future monitoring. It is an independent field assessment rather than a statutory waterbody classification.
