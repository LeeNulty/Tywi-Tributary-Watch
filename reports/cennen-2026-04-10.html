Summary

Two exploratory kick samples were taken approximately 50 metres apart: one from the River Cennen and one from the River Tywi. The aim was to obtain an initial indication of ecological health and compare the two rivers.

The Cennen sample indicated low ecological quality. Only two invertebrate indicator groups were observed, dominated by midge larvae; stoneflies, which are sensitive to pollution, were notably absent.

Findings from the River Cennen

The observed mix of organisms and the silty condition of the riverbed are consistent with ecological stress. Silk weed covered the river edges and fine silt lay across shallow areas, which can reduce habitat quality for invertebrates.

These observations may be consistent with nutrient enrichment, lower dissolved oxygen and/or organic or sediment pollution. Possible contributing pressures include farm run-off, fertiliser use and other upstream organic inputs. This survey alone cannot identify a source or establish a formal water-quality status.

Method and limits

Each sample was a one-minute exploratory kick sample. This is a useful field indication but is not a full biological assessment. Conditions can change with season, flow, weather, recent rainfall and the precise sampling location.

What next?

Repeat sampling at the same location across seasons and compare it with other Tywi tributaries. Recording water level, weather, photographs and basic chemical readings alongside future samples will strengthen the evidence.
